TinyFish is a web agent tool that allows you to deploy agents to browse the live web, just like a human would. Instead of relying on static datasets or APIs, it interacts with real websites in real time.

The hackathon challenge was simple: use TinyFish to build something interesting.

### Our Idea

We figured a lot of teams would build things like sentiment analyzers, web scrapers, or travel itinerary generators. Cool ideas, but we wanted something closer to our own lives.

So we asked ourselves a simple question:

> what is something we struggle with right now?

The answer was obvious: **adulting**.

Things like:
- choosing the right credit card  
- researching BTO launches  
- understanding CPF and financial decisions  

All of these require *a lot* of manual research. You open multiple tabs, compare different sources, read conflicting advice, and still end up unsure what actually applies to you.

We wanted to build something that cuts through all of that.

---

### The Idea in One Line

> What if an agent could do all that research for you, and just tell you what makes sense?

---

### Introducing: adulting.sg

adulting.sg is a personalised financial co-pilot for young adults in Singapore.

Instead of giving generic advice, it:
- looks at your actual profile (income, spending, preferences)
- browses the web in real time using TinyFish
- returns answers that are tailored to you

Think of it like having a really efficient version of yourself that:
- opens all the tabs  
- reads everything  
- filters the noise  
- and comes back with a clear answer  

---

### What We Built

We focused on three main features:

#### 💳 Credit Card Recommender
Instead of static “best cards” lists, the agent:
- browses sites like MoneySmart and SingSaver live  
- filters cards based on your income and eligibility  
- compares benefits against your spending habits  
- explains pros, cons, and trade-offs  

So instead of:
> “Here are the top cards”

You get:
> “These are the cards that actually make sense for you, and here’s why.”

---

#### 🏠 BTO Finder
Tracking BTO launches manually is painful.

Our agent:
- pulls upcoming HDB projects  
- maps them across Singapore  
- ranks them based on your preferred location, budget, and flat type  
- shows what you are eligible for, including grants  

It turns a messy research process into something straightforward.

---

#### 🤖 Ask Anything
This is our general-purpose research agent.

You can ask questions like:
- “How much CPF do I need before buying a flat?”  
- “What grants can I qualify for?”  
- “How does income tax work for me?”  

The agent:
- browses official sources like CPF, HDB, IRAS, and MoneySense  
- extracts relevant information  
- answers based on your profile  

Not guesses. Not generic explanations. Actual context-aware answers.

---

### Why This Matters

Most tools today fall into two categories:
- dashboards that show you data but expect you to interpret it  
- articles that give generic advice that may not apply to you  

Both require you to do the hardest part: turning information into a decision.

We wanted to flip that.

adulting.sg is built to answer:
> given your situation, what actually makes sense?

---

### Privacy by Design

One thing we were very clear about:
- everything runs locally  
- no accounts  
- no backend  
- your data stays on your device  

If something is helping you make financial decisions, it should not be quietly collecting your data.

---

### What We Learned

Building this in 8 hours was intense, but also eye-opening.

A few takeaways:
- browser automation + personal context is surprisingly powerful  
- the hardest part is not getting data, but making it *useful*  
- people do not want more information, they want clearer decisions  

---

### What’s Next

There is a lot more we want to explore:
- CPF projections and long-term planning  
- budgeting and lifestyle insights  
- “what-if” simulations (e.g. saving scenarios, housing affordability)  

This was just a starting point.
